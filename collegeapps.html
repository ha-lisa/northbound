Applying to College

Every college require different things. Some require letters of recommendations, SAT Subject Test scores, portfolios, interviews, etc. and some do not. Researching the colleges you want to attend is really important.

  1. Personal and demographic information -
  2. SAT/ACT Tests - Many colleges require these scores, although more and more are making them optional. You can send your scores on your Collegeboard account. Click here to learn how to send your scores.
  3. Additional Tests - Complete all the testing you need to do (SAT Subject Tests, AP, IB, TOEFL etc.). Do the SAT/ACT at least month before the deadline on college applications! This will save a lot of stress.
  4. Personal statement - Write your personal statement and have it reviewed by multiple people. You can find good examples at . If you hAve trouble starting, then check out . College websites are also great places to find tips directly from the admissions officers that will be reviewing your essay.
  5. Complete your Common Application and/or Coalition profiles if you are applying to colleges that are on those platforms.
  6. Decide on which colleges to apply for (go for at least 4, include at least a safe school, a target school, and a dream school) and finish those applications! You can often get fee waivers for those applications.
