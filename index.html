Coursework

Taking challenging courses and earning good grades looks good to colleges because they can see that you can succeed on their campus.

There are many types of advanced classes offered in the United States. It depends on your school on which of these classes are offered.

IMPORTANT: Even if your school does not offer many advanced or the types of classes you need to take to fulfill a college application requirement, you can still do 2 things: (1) Seek out outside resources and opportunities OUTSIDE what your school offers. This is good because it shows to colleges that you are resourceful and proactive. You can find resources to help you seek out these opportunities here. (2) In the "additional information" section of college applications, you can explain your situation, so that the college admissions person can see your transcript in context of what you were able to take in your school.
