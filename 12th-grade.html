NINTH GRADE TENTH GRADE ELEVENTH GRADE TWELFTH GRADE


Objective

You made it! The last grade in high school! This is YOUR year to make everything you have done up to this point count!




What you need to do

  • Continue taking academically challenging courses and MAINTAIN your grades!
  • Continue doing clubs/sports, volunteering, working at a job/internship and/or do other extracurricular activities. Continue documenting it and take up leadership positions in your activities.
  • Continue planning/attending sponsored events/conferences at univerisities/colleges.
  • Continue applying for scholarships. This is the year for those senior-specific scholarships!
  • Register for the FAFSA/WAFSA ASAP when it opens (Create your FAFSA ID before it opens and have all your information ready to go).
  • Lastly, have the TIME of your HIGH SCHOOL LIFE! It's senior season, so don't forget to maintain your friendships with your teachers and peers before you graduate and have less opportunities to see them.



Applying to College

Every college require different things. Some require letters of recommendations, SAT Subject Test scores, portfolios, interviews, etc. and some do not. Researching the colleges you want to attend is important.

  • Complete all the testing you need to do (SAT/ACT, SAT Subject Tests, AP, IB, etc.). Do the SAT/ACT at least month before the deadline on college applications! This will save a lot of stress.
  • Finish writing your personal statement and have it reviewed by multiple people.
  • Complete your Common Application and/or Coalition profiles if you are applying to colleges that are on those platforms.
  • Decide on which colleges to apply for (go for at least 4, include at least a safe school, a target school, and a dream school) and finish those applications! You can often get fee waivers for those applications.
